# Exchange-Correlation Functionals

## Overview

LYNX evaluates exchange-correlation (XC) functionals via [libxc](https://www.tddft.org/programs/libxc/). The XC energy and potential relate as:

$$
E_{\text{xc}} = \int \rho(\mathbf{r}) \, \varepsilon_{\text{xc}}[\rho](\mathbf{r}) \, d\mathbf{r}
$$

$$
V_{\text{xc}}(\mathbf{r}) = \frac{\delta E_{\text{xc}}}{\delta \rho(\mathbf{r})}
$$

## LDA (Local Density Approximation)

$$
\varepsilon_{\text{xc}} = \varepsilon_{\text{xc}}(\rho)
$$

Supported parameterizations:
- **LDA_PZ**: Slater exchange + Perdew-Zunger 1981 correlation
- **LDA_PW**: Slater exchange + Perdew-Wang 1992 correlation

The potential is simply:
$$
V_{\text{xc}} = \varepsilon_{\text{xc}} + \rho \frac{\partial \varepsilon_{\text{xc}}}{\partial \rho}
$$

## GGA (Generalized Gradient Approximation)

$$
\varepsilon_{\text{xc}} = \varepsilon_{\text{xc}}(\rho, |\nabla\rho|^2)
$$

Supported functionals:
- **GGA_PBE**: Perdew-Burke-Ernzerhof (1996)
- **GGA_PBEsol**: PBE revised for solids
- **GGA_RPBE**: Revised PBE

The GGA potential has two contributions:

$$
V_{\text{xc}} = \frac{\partial(\rho \varepsilon_{\text{xc}})}{\partial \rho}\bigg|_\sigma - \nabla \cdot \left( \frac{\partial(\rho \varepsilon_{\text{xc}})}{\partial \sigma} \cdot 2\nabla\rho \right)
$$

where $\sigma = |\nabla\rho|^2$ is the squared gradient. The libxc convention stores $v_\sigma = \partial(\rho\varepsilon_{\text{xc}})/\partial\sigma$.

### GGA Gradient in Non-Orthogonal Cells

The squared gradient uses the metric tensor:

$$
\sigma = |\nabla\rho|^2 = \sum_{\alpha\beta} G_{\alpha\beta} \frac{\partial\rho}{\partial\xi_\alpha} \frac{\partial\rho}{\partial\xi_\beta}
$$

where $G_{\alpha\beta}$ is the Laplacian metric tensor from the lattice vectors.

### GGA Divergence Term

The divergence correction $-\nabla \cdot (v_\sigma \cdot 2\nabla\rho)$ is computed as:

1. Compute $\mathbf{f} = v_\sigma \cdot \mathbf{G} \cdot \nabla\rho$ (in non-Cartesian coordinates)
2. Apply divergence: $V_{\text{xc}} \mathrel{-}= \nabla \cdot \mathbf{f}$

The divergence uses the FD gradient operator applied to $f_x, f_y, f_z$ components, then sums contributions.

## Spin-Polarized XC

For collinear spin, the XC functional depends on $\rho_\uparrow$ and $\rho_\downarrow$ separately:

$$
\varepsilon_{\text{xc}} = \varepsilon_{\text{xc}}(\rho_\uparrow, \rho_\downarrow)
$$

The potentials $V_{\text{xc},\uparrow}$ and $V_{\text{xc},\downarrow}$ are computed from libxc's spin-polarized interface.

For GGA, three gradient invariants are needed:
$$
\sigma_{\uparrow\uparrow} = |\nabla\rho_\uparrow|^2, \quad \sigma_{\downarrow\downarrow} = |\nabla\rho_\downarrow|^2, \quad \sigma_{\uparrow\downarrow} = \nabla\rho_\uparrow \cdot \nabla\rho_\downarrow
$$

### Density Threshold

To avoid numerical instabilities at very low densities (e.g., in vacuum regions), a floor is applied: $\rho \geq 10^{-14}$ before passing to libxc.

## NLCC in XC Evaluation

When nonlinear core correction is active, the core density is added before XC evaluation:

$$
V_{\text{xc}} = V_{\text{xc}}[\rho_{\text{val}} + \rho_c], \quad \varepsilon_{\text{xc}} = \varepsilon_{\text{xc}}[\rho_{\text{val}} + \rho_c]
$$

For spin-polarized: core charge is split equally between spins.
